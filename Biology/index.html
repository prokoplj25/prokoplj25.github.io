Huntington's disease

Definition of HUngingtons disease:

A genetic disorder that causes your nerves to progressively breakdown.

What happens to the body?

The nerves in the body begin to break down which causes sporadic movement, decline in thinking and reasoning skills, organization, mood swings, ect. Eventually this will lead to death or a state of unconsciousness>p>

What part of the body does it generally affect?

Brain and nervous system

All possible effects on the body

Involuntary jerking or writhing movements (chorea)Muscle problems, such as rigidity or muscle contracture (dystonia)Slow or abnormal eye movements.Impaired gait, posture and balance.Difficulty with speech or swallowing.difficulty concentrating.memory lapses.depression – including low mood, a lack of interest in things,, and feelings of hopelessness.stumbling and clumsiness.mood swings, such as irritability or aggressive behavior.

Cause of disorder

What happens in the body to cause the disease?

A mutation on the 4th chromosome which is dominant and codes for the huntington's protein.

How is it inherited?

Is it sex-linked?What type of genetic inheritance is it?

Caused by a single deficient gene on the 4th chromosome. The defect is dominant. Codes for the Huntington's protein.

How is the disorder treated and how is it diagnosed?

Physical therapy helps improve quality of life through prescribed exercise, hands-on care, and patient education. Xenazine helps with reducing sporadic and involuntary movements, Austedo helps reduce the implications that are from Huntington's disease.
